Wireless Capsule Endoscopy Images: Classification, Semantic Segmentation and Instance Segmentation
This was my first funded project in the biomedical sector. During the course of my internship, I was introduced to concepts of Deep Learning. I worked on classification, semantic segmentation, instance segmentation and clustering 9 selected abnormalities present in wireless capsule endoscopy (WCE) images. My experiements took into account different colour spaces, data augmentation methods, patching and coupling traditional clustering algorithms with DNNs for joint unsupervised learning. We worked in collaboration with the Gastroenterology Department at Command Hospital Bangalore.

Fast Bilateral Edge Detectors & Fast Steerable Bilateral Edge Detectors [Details] [Code] [IISc Presentation]
Initially, I implemented the algorithm on Fast Bilateral Filters using Fourier Kernels (this has a reasonable trade-off between image accuracy and computational time). Building on this algorithm, I designed a noise-robust and computationally efficient algorithm for fast bilateral edge detection using the concepts of steerability and bilateral edge dection. In span of 8 weeks, I designed two new algorithms based on fast bilateral edge detectors and steerable fast bilateral edge detectors. My algorithms have been used in a government funded project on sidewalk detection.

Transliteration of Kannada Text from a Camera Captured Scene-Image on an Android Platform [Details] [Code] [Presentation]
As a sophomore, I was lucky to get selected for an internship under Prof. KR Ramakrishnan. I designed a pre-processing algorithm for raw, textual scene images to suit the requirements of OCRs like Tesseract. Following this, I trained the Tesseract OCR separately, with combinations of various Kannada fonts for optimal recognition of the Kannada Script. I also tried my hand at convolutional neural networks for Kannada character recognition. Finally, I built an Android application that pre-processed the image and transliterated the textual content with the help of Tesseract tools for Android. I also used my pre-processing algorithm along with a PhD student's work on Kannada charater recognition. Our results(96.8%) surpassed the state of the art by almost 2%.

College Projects and Publications

Handwritten Hindi Numeral Recognition Using Clustering Techniques [Paper] [Link]
Hindi script is complex due to concavities, holes and curvatures. I evaluated several traditional and deep clustering techniques for Hindi numeral recognition. Subsequently, I modified the CNN modules in the recurrent framework in Joint Unsupervised Learning (JULE) of Deep Representations and Image Clusters. We obtained NMI values as high as 8.78 (surpassing pre-existing results). Additionally, I preprocessed images by de-noising and using contrast adjustment techniques.
Guide: Prof. Nisha Shetty

Brochure Design using Emotion Detection and Neural Style Transfer [Paper]
Although most corporates and private groups bank on various templates or human expertise for brochure designing, we wanted to automate the same. Based on a survey of 150 members, several textures from the DTD Data-set have been categorized based on the emotions they evoke. We train two emotion detection algorithms (based on ANNs and SVMs) on the Twitter Data-set and use the results for predicting the emotion associated with the catalogue content. Following this, a plain content image and a corresponding emotionally relevant texture image are combined via image style transfer. Our idea is novel; we couple concepts of image style transfer with textual images, evaluate the textual emotion detection methodologies and present designed brochures in our paper. Our paper is under review.
Guide: Prof.Nisha Shetty
Fun Fact: I learnt about neural style transfer from Serena Yeung's Stanford lectures. Prof.Subhransu Maji's DTD Dataset at UCB further inspired me!

Landslide Prediction Using Classifier Models [Paper] [Link]
To predict the occurence of landslides in different regions, we designed and evaluated classifier models like the Random Forest and Naive Bayes Classifiers. Our project required intensive data cleaning of raw data from NASA's Landslide Data-set. Following this, we visualised and analysed trends in parameters like rainfall, temperature, area, population etc.
