About Me

I was born and brought up in a broad-minded military family. Sound ethics and values, discipline, mental toughness and equitableness in our dealings are some of the aspects that have always been given distinct importance in my upbringing. Regardless of gender stereotyping in India, I have always been encouraged as a school student to participate in overnight school competitions and venture into mountaineering and scuba diving. Having seen and lived among the military of a diverse India, I call myself a product of a cross-cultural landscape. Spending a good many years of my life in Bangalore, the Silicon Valley of India, has helped me direct my passion for problem solving and mathematics to the realm of technology. Multidimensional influences have thus shaped my well-rounded personality.

My appreciation of interdisciplinary learning comes from my exposure to diverse fields at a young age. As a student, my weekends used to be fruitfully utilised in learning piano, painting, Bharatnatyam, Yoga, swimming and basketball. I actively participated in Sudoku competitions, math olympiads, debates and essay writing contests. Reading is my favourite childhood hobby and books continue to be my best companions to this day. In my free time, I enjoy grooming my pet dog.

At present, I am a research assistant at the Spectrum Lab in the Indian Institute of Science. I'm working on data augmentation of abnormal wireless capsule endoscopy (WCE) images using neural style transfer and pyramids. In addition to classification, I have previously worked on semantic segmentation, clustering and instance segmentation of abnormalities in WCE images. Prof. Chandra Sekhar Seelamantula is my principal investigator and our project is in collaboration with the Command Hospital Airforce, Bangalore.

Prior to this, I interned at the Indian Insitute of Science in my sophomore and junior year. In 2017, I designed two new algorithms at the Spectrum lab. The steerable bilateral edge detection and fast steerable bilateral edge detection algorithms were used in a real-time government funded project on footpath detection. As a sophomore, I designed an Android application which transliterates Kannada language text from a camera-captured scene image. Prof. K.R Ramakrishnan guided me for the same. In my seventh semester, I took up a remote project in the LISA Lab at IISc. For a duration of 2 months, I worked on 'shadow removal using bilateral filters'.

Over my undergraduate years at the Manipal Institute of Technology, I’ve had the privilege to be associated with a number of mentorship programs and technical clubs like IECSE, Google Developers’ Group, Women Techmakers Manipal and ACM- Women Manipal. Recently,I received my undergraduate degree in Computer and Communication Engineering.

Writing Corner

I love expressing my ideas in words. You never know how your learnings can inspire someone else. Here's where I pen down my experiences, introspect about current issues and elaborate on interesting research talks. I've been a writer at Coinmonks, IECSE and ACM-W Manipal.

Fast and Scalable Estimation of Uncertainty using Bayesian Deep Learning [Link]
Dr. Emtiyaz Khan's insights on fast computation of uncertainity and Variational ADAM. He walked us through variational inference, Bayesian models, natural gradients and fast Gaussian approximation for deep learning models.

Learn How to Train U-Net On Your Dataset [Link]
A step-by-step guide on how to train U-Net (since I found the original documentation too abstract). Several comments and issues on Github helped me in figuring out the training steps.

Graph Convolutional Neural Networks- A Talk at IISc [Link]
Dr. Tijmen Tieleman introduced us to the bleeding edge research in graph convolutional neural networks.

An IISc Seminar: The Beginning of VNIR [Link]
A startup centric talk conducted by Dr. Meher Prakash. He sheds light on his journey from CalTech and ETH Zurich to founding VNIR Biotechnologies Pvt Ltd.

An IISc Lecture: Deep Learning Research- Representation Learning [Link]
Prof. Sargur Narasimhamurthy Srihari touched upon various topics including the significance of representation, representation learning methods, transfer learning, disentangling variables and explainable AI.

A Beginner’s Guide to Installing OpenCV Android in Android Studio [Link]
Steps to integrate OpenCV Android with Android Studio (for newbies).
